Piyanggang Manok: The Rich and Smoky Grilled Chicken of Mindanao

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into serving parts
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • thumb-sized ginger, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp roasted ground rice (optional, for thickening)
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce or salt
  • 1 tbsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Steps:

  1. Marinate the chicken in turmeric, soy sauce, and fish sauce for about 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and saut√© onions, garlic, ginger, and bell pepper until soft.
  3. Add the marinated chicken, cook until browned.
  4. Pour in coconut milk and simmer until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce thickens.
  5. Optional: Add ground roasted rice to thicken the sauce.
  6. Serve with steamed rice.

The Smoky and Spicy Flavor Profile

  1. Smoky: The grilled chicken imparts a distinctive smoky flavor that sets the base of the dish. Grilling over an open flame or charcoal is an essential part of the process, as it infuses the chicken with that irresistible aroma.
  2. Spicy: The heat from the chili provides a vibrant, zesty contrast to the creaminess of the coconut milk. The level of spice can be adjusted based on your preference, but traditionally, Piyanggang Manok has a moderate level of heat.
  3. Creamy: The coconut milk and cream sauce gives the dish a rich, velvety texture, complementing the smoky and spicy flavors. The turmeric adds an earthy, aromatic quality, giving the dish depth and complexity.
