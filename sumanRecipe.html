Suman sa Lihiya: A Traditional Mindanao Rice Cake

Suman sa Lihiya is a beloved Filipino delicacy made from glutinous rice, cooked with lye water (lihiya), and wrapped in banana leaves. Known for its soft and chewy texture, this traditional treat is a popular snack or dessert in Mindanao, especially during festivals, celebrations, or family gatherings. The combination of glutinous rice and lye water gives the suman its unique flavor and distinct texture, making it one of the most cherished rice cakes in the Philippines.

While suman is enjoyed throughout the Philippines, Suman sa Lihiya has a particular place in the hearts of Mindanaoans, where it is often served with sugar, coconut, or syrup for added sweetness. Its simplicity and versatility make it a favorite treat, enjoyed by people of all ages.

What is Suman sa Lihiya?

Suman sa Lihiya is a type of glutinous rice cake that’s made with glutinous rice, lye water (lihiya), and sugar, wrapped in banana leaves for steaming. The lye water (also known as alkaline solution) is used to soften the rice and give it a slightly savory, unique taste. When steamed, the suman becomes sticky and chewy, making it a satisfying treat to eat.

The rice is first soaked in water, then mixed with lye water before being wrapped in banana leaves and steamed to perfection. The result is a flavorful, chewy rice cake that can be eaten on its own or paired with various sweet toppings, such as latik (coconut caramel), sugar, or mango.

Why Suman sa Lihiya is Special

Suman sa Lihiya is a special Filipino delicacy because it combines the rich flavors of glutinous rice and lye water, resulting in a chewy, satisfying dessert that is unique to Mindanao and other regions of the Philippines. It’s not just about the ingredients—it’s about the cultural significance of the dish. Suman is often made for festivals, holidays, and special gatherings, and it holds a place of tradition in Filipino kitchens.

The chewy texture of the suman, combined with the subtle savory-sweet flavor imparted by the lye water, makes it a one-of-a-kind rice cake that’s both comforting and delicious. The banana leaves give it an earthy aroma that adds to the overall experience of enjoying this traditional treat.

Why Try Suman sa Lihiya?

If you’re looking to explore Filipino rice cakes, Suman sa Lihiya is a must-try delicacy, especially if you’re curious about the traditional flavors of Mindanao. Its chewy texture, subtle savory-sweet flavor, and aromatic banana leaf wrapping make it a delightful treat that embodies the flavors of the region.

Whether you’re enjoying it as an afternoon snack, a dessert, or part of a festive meal, Suman sa Lihiya provides a comforting taste of Filipino heritage. The simplicity of its ingredients combined with its rich cultural significance makes it a truly special part of Mindanao’s culinary tradition.
