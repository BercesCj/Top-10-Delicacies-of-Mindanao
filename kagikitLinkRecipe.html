Kagikit: The Sweet Delight of Mindanao’s Southern Regions

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp peppercorns
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. Marinate the chicken in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and bay leaves for about 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pan, sauté onion and garlic until fragrant.
  3. Add the marinated chicken and cook until browned.
  4. Pour in coconut milk, peppercorns, and bay leaves. Simmer until the sauce reduces and thickens.
  5. Season with salt and pepper. Serve with rice.

Variations of Kagikit

While the basic ingredients remain relatively consistent, Kagikit can have regional variations based on available resources and local tastes:

  1. With Cornstarch: Some versions of Kagikit include cornstarch, which makes the texture smoother and firmer. This version is often easier to slice into neat portions.
  2. With Pandan or Vanilla Flavoring: In some regions, a hint of pandan or vanilla is added to enhance the flavor and aroma of Kagikit.
  3. Kagikit with Rice Flour: In areas where rice is abundant, rice flour is sometimes added to the mixture to provide a slightly more cake-like consistency.
