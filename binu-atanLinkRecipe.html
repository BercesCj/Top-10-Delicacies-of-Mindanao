Binu-atan: A Rich and Flavorful Sweet Dish from Mindanao

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter carabao milk (or substitute with full-fat cow's milk)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract

Steps:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the milk and sugar. Heat until it just starts to boil.
  2. Mix cornstarch with a little water to form a slurry, then add it to the milk while stirring constantly.
  3. Continue stirring until the mixture thickens.
  4. Add vanilla extract and mix well.
  5. Pour into molds and chill until set.
  6. Serve cold.

Variations of Binu-atan

Binu-atan may vary slightly from region to region, with local touches added to the dish depending on available ingredients and personal preferences. Here are some common variations:

  1. Binu-atan with Latik: Some variations of Binu-atan are topped with latik (coconut caramel), which is made by simmering coconut milk until the fat separates and turns into crispy golden bits. This addition adds a delightful crunch and extra flavor to the dish.
  2. Binu-atan with Fruits: Some modern adaptations of Binu-atan include the addition of fruits such as mangoes or bananas for added sweetness and variety. These fruits complement the rich coconut flavors, giving the dish a tropical twist.
  3. Binu-atan with Jackfruit: In some areas, jackfruit (langka) is added for additional flavor and texture. The fruit's sweet, floral taste pairs wonderfully with the creamy coconut and sticky rice.
